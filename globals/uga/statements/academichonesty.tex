All students at the University of Georgia agree to abide by the University's student honor code:
\begin{pullquote}
I will be academically honest in all of my academic work and will not tolerate academic dishonesty of others.
\end{pullquote}
The University's academic honesty policy \href{https://honesty.uga.edu/academic-honesty-policy/prohibited-conduct/}{prohibits certain conduct} among students, notably \textbf{plagiarism} and \textbf{unauthorized assistance}.
\begin{ul}
    \item Plagiarism is the presentation of another's work as one's own without proper citation. Students who quote or paraphrase the output of large language models (LLMs) when completing assignments in this course are expected to cite that output as they would cite any print or online source. Importantly, the citation should include a publicly accessible link to the AI conversation thread from which the student quotes or paraphrases the AI tool. Two examples are given below:
    \begin{pullquote}
    ChatGPT. Response to ``How to properly cite AI.'' OpenAI, May 24, 2026. \url{https://chatgpt.com/share/6a138f64-e90c-83ea-a17b-73c300ec8b8d}. \\
    Bing Image Creator. Response to ``Cityscape at sunset in cubist style.'' Microsoft, August 26, 2024. \url{https://th.bing.com/th/id/OIG1.giyozqMtQv0gACrZ2CO\_?pid=ImgGn}.
    \end{pullquote}
    For more information on how to cite AI in academic work, refer to \href{https://libguides.mcmaster.ca/cite-gen-ai/chicago}{``How Do I Cite Generative AI?: Chicago (18th ed.)''} on the McMaster University Libraries website.
    \item Unauthorized assistance is the giving or receiving of help without prior approval from the course instructor. When working on any graded work that is \emph{NOT} an exam, students may work with each other, use lecture notes and other materials on the course eLC page, or consult online resources, \textbf{\emph{including AI tools}}. However, each student is expected to submit work that is their own for all graded assignments. During exams, giving or receiving assistance from other students or any other party (e.g., AI tools or Internet-connected devices like smart watches) is expressly prohibited.
\end{ul}

The penalty for the first instance of prohibited conduct is a grade of zero on the affected assignment. A second instance of prohibited conduct results in a grade of F for the course. Please note that prohibited conduct under the University's academic honesty policy is not limited to only instances of plagiarism and unauthorized assistance.
